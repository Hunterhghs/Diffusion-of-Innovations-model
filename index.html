Rogers' Theory (1962)
The Diffusion of Innovations explains how new ideas and technologies spread through societies via a predictable adoption lifecycle. In development economics, this framework maps how transformative technologies — mobile banking, solar energy, digital payments — reach billions.
— Everett M. Rogers, "Diffusion of Innovations" (5th ed., 2003)
